Our Heritage

1985

The First Steamer

It began not with a grand opening, but with the gentle hiss of a single bamboo steamer in a small shophouse in Yaowarat. Grandmother Lin didn't believe in shortcuts. She believed that the dough had a spirit, one that needed to be coaxed awake with patience and warmth.

Her "Emperor's Pork Bun" was a secret whispered between flour and yeast, a recipe that demanded a full 12 hours of fermentation before the sun even touched the sky.

2010

East Meets West

As the neighborhood changed, so did our palate. The second generation brought home stories—and techniques—from French patisseries. We asked a simple question: Could the comfort of a cookie coexist with the sanctity of a salapao?

The answer was found in the "Mom's Oven" collection. Crisp, buttery textures that honored Western tradition, yet utilized the same meticulous sourcing principles Grandmother Lin established decades prior.
